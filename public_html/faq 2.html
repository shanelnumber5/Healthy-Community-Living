About the Program

  1. What is the Healthy Community Living Program?

    HCL is a program that supports opportunities for all people to live well and participate in their communities. It includes two separate but complementary peer-led workshops: Community Living Skills and Living Well in the Community. CLS provides introductory topics about living independently in the community. LWC guides participants to set and achieve personal life goals by adopting healthy lifestyle choices to help get them there. Read more information about the program and the history of its research and development.

  2. What do I need to use the HCL Program?

    You will need an HCL program license, someone to facilitate the workshops, internet access, a large screen or TV to project the HCL program workshop websites, and accessible space to conduct your workshops.

  3. What is included with a HCL license?

    i. Access to the HCL program for one year for up to 5 individuals per license.
    ii. Access to two workshops (CLS and LWC).
    iii. Access to facilitator trainings for both CLS and LWC, and an additional Facilitating Groups Training.
    iv. Access to peer support and technical assistance to implement the HCL program.
    v. The license is renewable on a yearly basis.
    vi. See license agreement for more details.

  4. What is the cost of the license?

    An annual HCL license is $500.00 (US Dollars). If an organization wishes to have more than 5 people access the HCL Program, it can purchase additional licenses to grant more access to more people associated with its organization through the license management page.

  5. How do I get a HCL Program license?

    You can purchase an annual license online.

  6. Do you offer any program discounts?

    We respect organizations’ differences in ability to pay and accept reduced fee license requests and review them for consideration. We reserve the right to grant or decline such requests per our own program maintenance, support and sustainability costs as a non-profit organization ourselves.

  9. How do I get a receipt?

    Receipts are not automatically sent out. If you need a receipt please email healthycommunityliving@mso.umt.edu with the date you purchased your license and we will forward you a copy of your receipt.

  10. Does my organization have to fulfill any requirements to purchase a license?

    i. You do not need to meet a specific requirement to obtain a license.

    ii. Workshop facilitators are expected to submit a brief report to RTC:Rural about their HCL Program activities to assist with program tracking, such as number of workshops completed, number of participants in the workshops, and other information. See reporting form for more details.

  11. Who can purchase a license for my organization?

    It is advised that a higher level administrator purchases the license; however, it is up to the organization to decide. Ideally, it is someone who can monitor and manage website users as well as respond to prompts to renew the license.

Training

  1. How is someone trained to run HCL program workshops?

    i. The HCL Program includes an online facilitator training for each workshop curriculum. Upon completion of the training, individuals can confidently conduct and facilitate workshops. The HCL facilitator training websites include helpful resources for planning, organizing, and conducting the workshops.

    ii. The facilitator website is NOT a train-the-trainer course.

    iii. The facilitator website is NOT for workshop participants to use.

  2. How does the facilitator website work?

    Individuals sign onto the facilitator website with their own unique individual logins. They proceed through the self-guided presentations and video tutorials at their own pace. Through the website, trainees learn about each HCL curriculum content and receive tips and recommendations on how to facilitate them with workshop participants.

  3. Is there other training support for facilitators?

    Yes, facilitators will be invited to participate in quarterly technical assistance webinars on various topics. These webinars will be provided at no additional charge as part of the license agreement.

  4. If my organization had given HCL Program access to facilitators under an expired license; do they have to be assigned under a new license to maintain access to the program?

    Yes, the annual HCL license is for program access, and is not a training fee. All facilitators who will conduct workshops under an organization’s license need a designated access slot regardless of whether or not they have completed the facilitator trainings in the past.  

License Renewals

  1. Does the license ever expire?

    ​Yes. The license is valid for one year from the date of purchase. License holders will get reminders to renew before expiration.  When an organization’s license expires, access to the HCL Program and materials are blocked.

  2. What happens if my organization’s license expires?

    If your organization’s license expires, you will lose access to the HCL Program. An organization can purchase a new license at any time.

  3. How much does a license renewal cost?

    License renewals are $500.00 (US Dollars) and includes the same permissions as outlined in the previous license unless changes or updates to the license are otherwise noted by RTC:Rural.

Being a Facilitator

  1. How do I get access as a workshop facilitator?

    First, an organization must obtain a license to access the HCL Program. Once licensed, the organization has permission to assign people access to the HCL Program with unique individual logins. 

  2. Once I am trained as a facilitator, can I train other people to be facilitators?

    No, this is not a train-the-trainer website. Facilitators are only trained to conduct workshops with participants, not to train others to deliver the program or conduct workshops.

  3. If our organization has a volunteer or peer advocate co-facilitating a workshop but we have used our five facilitator slots for staff, will we be required to purchase another license for that peer?

    No. For both volunteers, and any workshop participants who wish to access the workshops outside of class hours, you may share the log-in for the facilitator in charge of conducting the workshop.

  4. How do I get certified as an HCL facilitator?

    Once you have gone through the entirety of the self-study facilitator website for the workshop you plan on facilitating (either CLS or LWC), you may print a certificate from the facilitator site's Resources Session, in the Certificates Tab.

Program Materials

  1. Are there print materials with the HCL Program?
    ​
    Each workshop curriculum has printable support materials for participants to use, and the content in each of the curriculum website slideshows are also printable.

    ​The worksheets are also available in electronic formats, so consumers who prefer to fill out forms on a computer or other device may do so.

    There is NOT a book or manual with any HCL workshop.
